# EV Interest Tracker — Project Guide

## What this is
A daily-updated news tracker monitoring global EV interest, fuel prices, and policy responses to the 2026 oil crisis (Iran/Hormuz disruption). Static site deployed on Cloudflare Pages.

Live URL: https://evinterest.pages.dev
Custom domain: https://ev.energysada.com

## Country News Feed schema
| Column | Field | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| A | Date | YYYY-MM-DD, article publication date |
| B | Region | North America, Europe, Asia, Asia-Pacific, Central Asia, Global |
| C | Country | Country name |
| D | Category | One of the 10 categories above |
| E | Signal/Headline | Source-prefixed headline |

## Daily refresh pipeline

The scheduled task lives at `~/.claude/scheduled-tasks/ev-interest-country-scan/SKILL.md`.

Steps:
1. **Search** — Web search for EV/fuel price news across all tiers (last 24h)
2. **Deduplicate** — Read existing Excel rows, compare headlines (first 80 chars lowercase)
3. **Append to Excel** — Use openpyxl. Hyperlink the URL in column H. Save.
4. **Update Indicators** — `python3 scripts/update_indicators.py` (Google Trends, Brent, date labels)
5. **Regenerate JSON** — `python3 scripts/excel_to_json.py`
6. **Deploy** — `PATH="/opt/homebrew/bin:$PATH" npx wrangler pages deploy . --project-name evinterest`
7. **Save summary** — Write `daily-scan-YYYY-MM-DD.md` to the Google Drive `data/` folder

## Rules
- When citing growth/change numbers, always specify the baseline (vs what period, vs what level). Never say "+36%" without "since Feb 28" or "YoY" or "MoM" etc. Pre-war (Feb 28) is the default benchmark.
- Don't ask before deploying — just deploy.
- **Tone / BNEF short:** Audience is industry professionals and investment community. Language should sit between journalist and academic, leaning academic. No advocacy language — we are not trying to prove EVs are winning. Avoid strong/editorializing language ("Banks don't double loan volumes on curiosity"). State the data and let the reader draw conclusions.
- **Clarity over brevity:** Don't sacrifice clarity to be terse. If a bullet is unclear about what metric is being measured, expand it rather than cutting words. Better to include fewer points with full context than many compressed points that confuse the reader. Always specify what the number measures (e.g. "EV purchase loan approvals" not "lending volumes").
- **Counterarguments section:** Actively populate and maintain. Flag weak signals, markets where interest declined, and structural barriers. This section builds credibility.
- **Framing:** Use "global energy crisis" not "Iran/Hormuz crisis" or "oil shock." Use "short term" not "cyclical" — this crisis is unprecedented, not a recurring pattern. Frame structural shifts neutrally — "showing multiple signs of long term and structural shifts with implications for EV demand" not "this isn't just search curiosity."
- **No journalistic color language** in the BNEF short. No "selling off the boat", "golden window", "tipping point" etc. Use the number instead. If AU dealers are charging over list, say "AU dealer premiums of AU$8-10K above MSRP reported". If NZ EV sales surged, say "+286% YoY". Always prefer the quantitative expression over the qualitative metaphor.
- **Specify the noun.** Not "supply constraints appearing" — say "EV supply constraints" or "new BEV inventory shortages". The reader should never have to infer what category of thing is being discussed.
- **Cross-reference evsales tracker daily.** Read `/Users/energysada/ev/evsales-site/index.html` STATUS array for the `swing` field. Markets flagged `swing:"up"` are where March EV sales growth likely breaks pre-existing trends (energy crisis impact). Currently: France, Sweden, Australia. Markets flagged `swing:"watch"` need more evidence (Germany, Denmark, China). Include crisis-flagged markets in the BNEF short executive summary and indicators table.
- **Distinguish government policy from consumer demand.** Frontier markets enacting EV-supportive policy (Cambodia, Uzbekistan, Pakistan, Laos) is a sign of governments responding to the oil crisis by accelerating EV frameworks — not necessarily evidence of organic consumer demand. Caveat accordingly. A policy timeline is worth maintaining as a recurring feature in the BNEF short.
- Dedup is headline-based — compare first 80 chars lowercase of column E.
- npx/node require PATH prefix — always use `PATH="/opt/homebrew/bin:$PATH"` before npx commands.
- meta.json `date` and `last_updated` auto-set to today by excel_to_json.py.
- meta.json `brent` and `brent_change` are static fallbacks — live price comes from `/api/brent` (Cloudflare Function).
- commentary.json (Key Highlights) is manually edited by Sada.
- Gas price % change in indicators always anchors to pre-war baseline (Feb 28).
- Google Trends % change anchors to Feb 25 baseline.
- yoy.xlsx is proprietary — never expose benchmark numbers on the public site.
